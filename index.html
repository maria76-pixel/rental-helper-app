Your Guaranteed Tenant Rights

Rights your landlord must respect — whether or not they tell you

▼
01

Right to Habitable Conditions

Your landlord must maintain safe and livable conditions — working heat, plumbing, and no pest infestations. This cannot be waived even if the contract says otherwise.

02

Right to Privacy

Landlords must give reasonable advance notice (typically 24–48 hours) before entering your unit, except in genuine emergencies.

03

Right Against Illegal Clauses

Any clause that waives your legal rights — such as "landlord is never responsible for repairs" — is unenforceable by law, even if you signed it.

04

Right to Security Deposit Return

Your deposit must be returned within the legally required period after moving out, with an itemized list of any deductions. Landlords cannot keep it without justification.

05

Right Against Retaliation

A landlord cannot evict you, raise your rent, or reduce services because you reported a problem or exercised a legal right. This protection is guaranteed by law.
